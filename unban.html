Required Information

Your appeal ticket must include all of the following:

Minecraft Username

Your exact Minecraft username (case-sensitive)

Discord ID

Your full Discord username and discriminator (e.g., User#1234)

Ban Reason

As shown when trying to join the server

Ban Date

Approximate date you were banned

Detailed Explanation

What happened from your perspective (be honest)

Evidence

Screenshots or videos that support your case

Automatic Denials

Appeals will be automatically denied if they:

  • Contain false information or misrepresentation
  • Have insufficient evidence
  • Show no acceptance of responsibility
  • Attempt to blame others without proof
  • Are incomplete or rushed
  • Are submitted through any method other than Discord tickets

Appeal Process

What Happens After Submission?

  1. Ticket Creation: Your appeal ticket will be assigned to staff
  2. Initial Review: Staff will verify your information within 3-7 days
  3. Evidence Check: Your provided evidence will be thoroughly examined
  4. Decision: You'll receive a response in your Discord ticket
  5. If Approved: Your ban will be lifted with possible restrictions
  6. If Denied: You'll receive reasons and can appeal again after waiting period
Processing Times

Average response times based on ban type:

  • Cheating/Hacking: 7-14 days (3-month minimum wait)
  • Toxicity: 3-7 days
  • Bug Exploits: 5-10 days
  • Discord Bans: 2-5 days
Important Notes
  • Do not ping or DM staff about your appeal status - this may result in denial
  • Duplicate appeals will be automatically rejected
  • Bans older than 1 year may require additional verification
  • All decisions are final but you may re-appeal after the waiting period

Ban Policy Reference

Common Ban Reasons & Durations

  • Cheating/Hacking: Permanent (first offense)
  • Toxicity/Harassment: 30 days to permanent (based on severity)
  • Bug Exploitation: 14 days to permanent
  • Account Sharing: 30 day suspension
  • Impersonation: Permanent
  • Discord Rules Violation: 7 days to permanent

Successful Appeal Conditions

Your appeal is more likely to succeed if:

  1. It's your first offense
  2. You provide compelling evidence of innocence
  3. You demonstrate genuine understanding of the rules
  4. You show clear effort to prevent future issues
  5. You've waited the appropriate time before appealing
